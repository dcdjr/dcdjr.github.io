About

A practical approach to systems and security engineering.

I am a Computer Science student at the University of Rhode Island and a Schilling Scholar in the Honors College. I am most interested in software that demands careful reasoning about behavior, reliability, and performance.

Current direction

Building depth in the fundamentals

I am intentionally choosing work that strengthens my understanding of processes, protocols, memory, and system behavior.

How I approach engineering

CS50 provided a strong introduction to computer science and confirmed that I learn best by building. Since then, I have continued toward projects that expose the mechanics beneath higher-level abstractions.

I have worked in C, C++, Python, and SQL, and I contributed to a research project with the Rhode Island Air National Guard comparing VR-based military training with more traditional approaches. That combination of implementation and analysis has strengthened how I think about evidence, tradeoffs, and technical communication.

My long-term goal is to build secure, reliable software close to the system layer, where careful design and strong fundamentals matter.
