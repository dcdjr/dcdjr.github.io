About

I like software that rewards patience, debugging, and real understanding.

I’m a Computer Science student at the University of Rhode Island and a Schilling Scholar in the Honors College. The work that pulls me in most lives close to the machine: systems programming, networking, cybersecurity, and the details that make software trustworthy.

Right now

Building depth, not just surface familiarity

I’m deliberately choosing projects that make process control, sockets, memory, and architecture feel concrete.

How I think about engineering

Harvard’s CS50 gave me a strong start and made it clear that I enjoy learning by building. Since then, I’ve kept gravitating toward projects that strip away convenience and make me work through how software really behaves.

I’ve built in C, C++, Python, and SQL, and I’ve also worked with the Rhode Island Air National Guard on a research project comparing VR-based military training with more traditional approaches. That mix of implementation and applied analysis has pushed me to care not just about whether something works, but why it works.

Long term, I want to work where security and real systems meet. I’m motivated by the idea of building software that is thoughtful, resilient, and grounded in a real understanding of the underlying stack.
