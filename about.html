Background

I’m Daniel “DJ” DiPietro Jr., a Computer Science student at the University of Rhode Island with a strong interest in systems programming, networking, and security.

A lot of my work is centered around building software closer to the underlying system. I’m especially interested in Linux, process behavior, sockets, low-level debugging, and the kinds of problems that force you to understand what the machine is actually doing.

Outside the classroom, I work as a Student Information Technologist at URI Memorial Union IT and serve as a Technical Content Lead for the URI Cybersecurity Club. Those roles have pushed me to explain technical ideas clearly, troubleshoot real systems, and keep building beyond coursework.

I use projects to understand systems better, not just to stack tools on a resume. I care about writing software that is simple, correct, and grounded in how systems really behave.
